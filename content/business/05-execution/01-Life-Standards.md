## PART 1: BODY STANDARDS

### Sleep (Foundation of Everything)

**Non-Negotiables**

- 7-8 hours of sleep (minimum 7, ideal 7.5-8)
- Bedtime: 22h00 max (digital sunset 20:30)
- Wake up: 06h30 (consistent, non-négociable)
- No screens 1.5h before bed (après 20:30)
- Room: Dark, cool (18-19°C), quiet

**Evening Protocol**

- 20:30 - Digital sunset (phone in another room)
- 20:30 - Light stretching or reading
- 21:30 - Journaling / Gratitude
- 22:00 - Lights out

**Morning Protocol**

- Wake - No snooze. Feet on floor immediately.
- +5min - Sunlight exposure (window or outside)
- +10min - Hydration (500ml water + electrolytes)

**Tracking**

- Sleep score /10 every morning
- HRV if available
- Energy level at 10am /10

Minimum acceptable: 7h sleep, energy 7/10

### Nutrition (O- Optimized)

**Daily Non-Negotiables**

- 2L+ water minimum
- Protein at every meal (target: 1.6-2g/kg = 105-130g/day)
- Vegetables at lunch AND dinner
- No processed sugar (occasional exception max 1x/week)
- No alcohol on work nights (max 2x/week social)
- Coffee: max 2 cups, none after 14h00

**O- Blood Type Preferences**

Prioritize: Lean proteins, fish, vegetables, fruits
Good: Eggs, olive oil, nuts, legumes (moderation)
Limit: Dairy, wheat, processed carbs
Avoid: Processed foods, refined sugar, excess alcohol

**Meal Structure**

- Breakfast: Protein-focused (eggs, greek yogurt, protein shake)
- Lunch: Protein + complex carbs + vegetables
- Dinner: Protein + vegetables (lighter carbs)
- Snacks: Nuts, fruits, protein if needed

**Supplements (to discuss with doctor)**

- Vitamin D (especially winter)
- Omega-3
- Magnesium (evening, helps sleep + ADHD)
- Creatine (for training)

**Body Composition Target**

Current: 65kg | Target: 68-72kg (lean muscle gain)
Timeline: 12-18 months
Method: Slight caloric surplus + progressive resistance training

### Exercise (Consistency > Intensity)

**Weekly Minimum**

- 4x resistance training (45-60 min)
- 2x cardio (20-30 min, can be walking)
- Daily: 8,000+ steps
- Daily: 5-10 min mobility/stretching

**Weekly Structure**

- Lundi: Upper Body (Push focus)
- Mardi: Lower Body
- Mercredi: Rest or Light Cardio/Walk
- Jeudi: Upper Body (Pull focus)
- Vendredi: Lower Body or Full Body
- Samedi: Active recovery (walk, sport fun, mobility)
- Dimanche: Rest

**Non-Negotiables**

- Never miss Monday (sets the tone for the week)
- Never miss 2 days in a row
- Something > Nothing (even 15 min counts)
- Progressive overload: increase weight/reps over time

**Tracking**

- Log every workout
- Weekly: Did I hit 4 resistance sessions?
- Monthly: Am I progressing? (weights, reps, form)

Minimum acceptable: 3x resistance + 10,000 steps/week average

### Aesthetics (Gentleman Standard)

**Daily Non-Negotiables**

- Shower (morning)
- Skincare routine (cleanser, moisturizer, SPF)
- Teeth brushed 2x + floss
- Deodorant
- Clean, fitted clothes
- Subtle fragrance

**Weekly**

- Nails trimmed and clean
- Beard groomed (if applicable)
- Eyebrows checked
- Wardrobe check (clothes ready for week)

**Monthly**

- Haircut (every 3-4 weeks)
- Wardrobe audit (anything to replace/upgrade?)
- Skincare products check

**Style Principles**

- Fit > Brand (well-fitted basics beat expensive ill-fitting)
- Quality basics: White tees, dark jeans, clean sneakers
- 1-2 statement pieces per outfit max
- Shoes always clean
- Subtle > Flashy (Gentleman, not showoff)

**Investment Pieces (Build over time)**

- Quality watch (classic, not flashy)
- Good leather shoes (1-2 pairs)
- Well-fitted blazer
- Quality sunglasses
- Good fragrance (signature scent)

## PART 2: MIND STANDARDS

### Mental Health (INFP + ADHD Optimized)

**Daily Non-Negotiables**

- 10 min minimum meditation/mindfulness
- Journaling (morning intention OR evening reflection)
- 1 moment of gratitude (written or mental)
- Time in nature or outside (even 15 min)
- No doom-scrolling (social media < 30 min/day)

**ADHD Management**

- Medication taken consistently (if prescribed)
- Tasks written down (not in head)
- One task at a time (no multitasking illusion)
- Pomodoro or time-blocking for deep work
- Environment optimized (minimal distractions)
- Movement breaks every 45-60 min

**INFP Energy Management**

- Solo recharge time daily (non-negotiable)
- Social events: Plan recovery time after
- Values alignment check: Am I living true to myself?
- Creative outlet (even 15 min)

**Emotional Regulation**

- Name the emotion when it arises
- No major decisions when emotional
- Physical outlet for intense emotions (walk, exercise)
- Talk to someone trusted when needed

**Weekly**

- 1 longer meditation session (20-30 min)
- Weekly review (wins, lessons, adjustments)
- Mental health check: How am I really doing?

Minimum acceptable: 10 min mindfulness + journaling daily

## PART 3: WORK STANDARDS

### Productivity (ADHD-Optimized)

**Daily Non-Negotiables**

- Morning routine 06:30-09:00 completed before work
- Top 3 priorities identified (MIT - Most Important Tasks)
- Deep work: 4-6h (pas 8h linéaire - Manifestor energy)
- No social media until after first deep work block
- End of day: Tomorrow's priorities set

**Work Structure**

- 06:30-07:00: Wake + Morning routine
- 07:00-07:30: Planning + Intention
- 07:30-09:30: DEEP WORK BLOCK 1 (highest priority)
- 09:30-10:00: Break + Movement
- 10:00-12:00: DEEP WORK BLOCK 2
- 12:00-13:00: Lunch + Rest
- 13:00-15:00: Meetings / Calls / Admin
- 15:00-17:00: Deep work or lighter tasks
- 17:00-17:30: Wrap up + Plan tomorrow
- 17:30+: Personal time

**ADHD Productivity Rules**

- External deadlines > Internal deadlines
- Accountability partner for big projects
- Break big tasks into tiny steps (15 min max each)
- Start with "just 5 minutes" when resistant
- Reward after completing hard tasks
- Environment matters: Work in a dedicated space
- Music/noise for focus (lo-fi, brown noise)

**Weekly Minimum**

- 20-30h deep work (4-6h × 5 jours)
- Progression sur les systemes signature CAIO Academy
- Cadence LinkedIn et newsletter respectee

Minimum acceptable: 3h deep work/day, Top 3 completed

## PART 4: SOCIAL STANDARDS

### Relationships (Gentleman Lover Philosophy)

**Core Philosophy**

"Real connections, not traditional relationships.
Intensity of moment > promises of forever.
Love freely, leave without hurting."

**Non-Negotiables in All Relationships**

- 100% transparency about intentions
- Never promise what you can't give
- Never ghost (clear communication always)
- Leave people better than you found them
- Give without expecting return
- Present when you're there (no half-presence)

**Dating Standards**

- Quality > Quantity
- Clear on what you want from the start
- No games, no manipulation
- Respect their time and feelings
- Don't date just to not be alone

**Red Flags (Walk away)**

- Games, tests, manipulation
- Drama seeking
- Dishonesty
- Tries to change you
- Doesn't respect your boundaries
- Energy vampire

### Friendships & Network

**Inner Circle (3-5 people)**

- Contact at least weekly
- Can be vulnerable with them
- Mutual support and growth
- No competition, only elevation

**Broader Network**

- Reach out to 2-3 contacts per week
- Give before asking (always)
- Connect people who should know each other
- Be genuinely interested in their work

**Network Growth (For business goals)**

- 1 new meaningful connection per week
- Attend 1-2 events per month
- Active in relevant communities online
- Provide value first, always

**Standards for Who You Spend Time With**

"You are the average of the 5 people you spend most time with"

Surround yourself with:
- Ambitious but grounded people
- People ahead of you in areas you want to grow
- Authentic, no-bullshit people
- People who challenge you
- Givers, not takers

Limit time with:
- Complainers and victims
- People who drain your energy
- Those who don't respect your time
- Negative influences

### Communication Standards

**Always**

- Listen more than you talk
- Ask questions, show genuine curiosity
- Be present (phone away, eye contact)
- Make others feel important
- Say what you mean, mean what you say
- Respond within 24h (or set expectation)

**Never**

- Interrupt
- One-up stories
- Give unsolicited advice
- Talk about yourself for more than 2 min straight
- Name-drop or status-signal
- Gossip or speak badly of others

**Gentleman Communication**

- Compliment genuinely, never flatter
- Criticize in private, praise in public
- Admit mistakes quickly, without excuses
- Share credit, take responsibility
- Be humble about success, open about failures

## PART 5: ENVIRONMENT STANDARDS

### Physical Space

**Workspace**

- Clean, organized desk (clear desk = clear mind)
- Good lighting (natural if possible)
- Minimal distractions visible
- Quality tools (good chair, monitor, keyboard)
- Plants or something alive

**Living Space**

- Clean and tidy (daily 10 min cleanup)
- Bed made every morning
- No clutter accumulation
- Intentional, not random (everything has a place)

**Weekly**

- Laundry done, clothes organized
- Clean bathroom and kitchen
- Meal prep or groceries planned

**Monthly**

- Deep clean
- Declutter one area
- Review what's not being used

**Principle**

"Your space reflects your mind.
A successful person lives in an intentional environment."

### Digital Environment

**Phone**

- Notifications: Only essential (calls, calendar, important apps)
- No social media on home screen
- Screen time limit: 2h max (non-work)
- Grayscale mode (optional, reduces dopamine hits)

**Social Media**

- Consumption: < 30 min/day
- Creation > Consumption
- Unfollow anything that doesn't add value
- No checking first thing in morning or before bed

**Email**

- Check 2-3x/day at set times (not constantly)
- Inbox zero weekly
- Unsubscribe ruthlessly

**Computer**

- Desktop clean (minimal icons)
- Browser tabs: Close what you're not using
- Files organized (system that you follow)
- Distracting sites blocked during work

**ADHD-Specific**

- Website blocker during deep work (Cold Turkey, etc.)
- One browser window at a time
- Phone in another room during deep work
- "Focus mode" on all devices

## The Daily Minimum (When Everything Falls Apart)

When you can't do everything, do this:

- 7h sleep minimum
- Move for 15 minutes (walk, stretch, anything)
- 1 healthy meal
- 1 MIT (Most Important Task) completed
- 5 minutes journaling or meditation
- No alcohol
- Reach out to 1 person (even just a text)

That's it. On bad days, this is success.
Protect the floor, not just the ceiling.
