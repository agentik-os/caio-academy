## Compliance & Legal (CRITICAL)

### February 2024 FCC Ruling

[!] **IMPORTANT**: FCC ruled that AI-generated voices in automated calls are "artificial" under TCPA

**Implications**:
- Must have prior express written consent for marketing calls
- Fines up to $1,500 per violation
- Class action lawsuit risk

**Requirements for Compliance**:
1. Obtain explicit consent before calling
2. Provide clear opt-out mechanism
3. Maintain do-not-call list
4. Disclose AI nature of caller
5. Record keeping for compliance

**Safe Use Cases** (No TCPA issues):
- Inbound customer support
- Appointment reminders (with consent)
- Internal business calls
- Lead qualification (after initial consent)

Source: [FCC AI Voice Ruling 2024](https://www.marketsandmarkets.com/AI-sales/voice-ai-can-agents-successfully-cold-call)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### [!] Pitfall 1: Over-Promising in Script

**Problem**: AI agent promises what it can't deliver

**Solution**: Conservative scripting, clear handoff to human when needed

---

### [!] Pitfall 2: No Fallback Logic

**Problem**: Agent gets stuck when conversation goes off-script

**Solution**: Build robust fallback responses, escalation to human

---

### [!] Pitfall 3: Ignoring Compliance

**Problem**: TCPA violations lead to lawsuits

**Solution**: Get written consent, maintain do-not-call list, disclose AI nature

---

### [!] Pitfall 4: Poor Voice Quality

**Problem**: Robotic or unnatural voice destroys trust

**Solution**: Invest in premium voices (ElevenLabs), test extensively

---

### [!] Pitfall 5: No Human Escalation

**Problem**: Frustrated prospects when AI can't help

**Solution**: Always provide easy path to human agent
