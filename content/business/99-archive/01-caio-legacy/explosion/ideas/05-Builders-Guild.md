## Le Probleme (Douleur du Marche)

### La Solitude du Builder

```
REALITE:
- Construire seul est dur
- 90% des "communautes" sont pleines de lurkers
- La plupart des "masterminds" sont des pitch fests
- Difficile de trouver des pairs de meme niveau

LE SENTIMENT:
"Je suis entoure de gens qui PARLENT de builder
mais qui ne BUILDENT jamais rien.
Je veux des gens qui FONT, pas qui DISENT."
```

## Structure de la Guilde

### Hierarchy

```
THE GUILD RANKS:

1. APPRENTICE (Entry level)
   +-- 0-$1K MRR ou projet lance
   +-- Learning the craft
   +-- Paired with a Journeyman mentor
   +-- 6 mois pour passer Journeyman

2. JOURNEYMAN (Proven builder)
   +-- $1K-$10K MRR ou 2+ projets lances
   +-- Can sponsor Apprentices
   +-- Full community access
   +-- 12 mois pour passer Master

3. MASTER (Established)
   +-- $10K+ MRR ou exit significatif
   +-- Can sponsor Journeymen
   +-- Priority access to everything
   +-- Speaker at Guild events

4. ELDER (Top tier)
   +-- $100K+ MRR ou multiple exits
   +-- Governance participation
   +-- Shapes Guild direction
   +-- Limited to 12 members

PROGRESSION:
- Based on RESULTS, not time
- Peer review + metrics
- Ceremonies for rank-up
```

### Application Process

```
APPLICATION FLOW:

1. SUBMIT APPLICATION
   +-- Portfolio: Links to what you've built
   +-- Revenue: Current MRR (can be $0 for new)
   +-- Motivation: Why the Guild?
   +-- Commitment: What will you build next?

2. REVIEW (48h)
   +-- Reviewed by 3 random members
   +-- Score: 1-10 on each criterion
   +-- Threshold: Average 7+

3. SPONSORSHIP
   +-- 2 existing members vouch for you
   +-- If no sponsors, waitlist for "Sponsor Pool"
   +-- Sponsors are responsible for your conduct

4. INITIATION
   +-- Pay $500 annual dues
   +-- Welcome ceremony (Discord call)
   +-- Paired with mentor (if Apprentice)
   +-- 30-day "build challenge"

REJECTION:
- Constructive feedback provided
- Can reapply in 6 months
- No hard feelings, just standards
```

### Why $500/year?

```
PRICING PSYCHOLOGY:

$500 =
- Enough to filter non-serious
- Not enough to exclude good builders
- Forces "is this worth it?" question
- Skin in the game
- Monthly: $42 (cup of coffee per week)

COMPARISON:
- Y Combinator: Free but 7% equity
- EO/YPO: $5K-20K/year
- Random masterminds: $200-500/month
- Online courses: $500-2000 one-time

VALUE DELIVERED: ~$25K in deals alone
ROI: 50x on membership fee
```

## Competitive Analysis

| Concurrent | Forces | Faiblesses | Notre Avantage |
|------------|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Indie Hackers** | Large, free | Diluted quality | We're curated |
| **YC Network** | Prestigious | Exclusive (need YC) | We're accessible |
| **On Deck** | Well-funded | Too big, too broad | We're focused |
| **Masterminds** | Personal | Expensive, inconsistent | We're systematic |

### Moat

```
1. CURATION (quality > quantity)
   - Strict entry
   - Active removal of inactive
   - Reputation matters

2. CULTURE (can't be copied)
   - Rituals, vocabulary, traditions
   - Takes years to build
   - Self-reinforcing

3. NETWORK EFFECTS
   - More members = more value
   - More sponsors = faster onboarding
   - More deals = more savings

4. ALUMNI EFFECT
   - Successful exits = prestige
   - Alumni give back
   - Brand compounds
```

|--------|--------|------------|
| **Quality dilution** | High | Strict criteria, active pruning |
