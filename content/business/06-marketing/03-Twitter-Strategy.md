## Account Positioning

### Bio Formula

The ideal bio follows a four-part formula: **Identity** plus **Credibility** plus **Value proposition** plus **Call to action.** For example: "Building AI tools for developers | From 0 to $100K MRR journey | Sharing everything I learn | DM for collabs."

### Profile Optimization

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|---------------|
| Name | Real name plus descriptor |
| Handle | Simple, memorable |
| Bio | Clear value plus credibility |
| Banner | Show products or personal brand |
| Pinned | Best performing tweet or launch announcement |
| Link | Newsletter or main product |

## Thread Structure

### Hook (Tweet 1)

The first tweet must stop the scroll. Follow this pattern: **Result plus Timeframe plus Promise.** Example: "I went from 0 to $10K MRR in 6 months. Here are 10 lessons that cost me $50K+ to learn:"

### Body (Tweets 2--9)

Each tweet in the body delivers one clear point in 280 characters or fewer. No fluff. Make each tweet actionable whenever possible. Every tweet should stand alone as a valuable insight even if someone never reads the rest of the thread.

### Close (Tweet 10)

End with a clear triple call to action: follow for more, retweet the first tweet for reach, and reply with your biggest takeaway. Reinforce the commitment: "Building in public. New thread every week."

## Hook Formulas

### Curiosity Gap

Open with a result, introduce a surprising obstacle, then promise the story: "I made $X doing Y. But it almost failed because of [surprising reason]. Here's what happened:"

### Contrarian

Lead with a bold disagreement: "Unpopular opinion: [Common belief] is wrong. Here's why:"

### Numbers

Leverage data and patterns: "I analyzed X [things]. Here are the Y patterns that separate winners from losers:"

### Problem-Solution

Start with empathy, then deliver the answer: "Most people struggle with X. After [time] of testing, I found what actually works:"

## Build in Public Framework

### What to Share

| Category | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Revenue | "Just crossed $X MRR" |
| Users | "Hit Y users this week" |
| Learnings | "Biggest lesson from this launch" |
| Failures | "This feature flopped. Here's why" |
| Decisions | "Why I chose X over Y" |
| Process | "My workflow for Z" |

### What NOT to Share

- Exact code or secrets that give competitive advantage
- Negative comments about specific people
- Unverified claims
- Sensitive user data

## Engagement Strategy

### Who to Engage With

| Account Type | Why |
|--------------|-----|
| Similar size | Mutual growth -- you lift each other |
| Bigger accounts | Visibility to larger audiences |
| Potential users | Market research and early feedback |
| Industry experts | Learning and relationship building |

### How to Engage

| Method | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Thoughtful reply | Add genuine insight, never just "great post" |
| Quote tweet | Add your own perspective and framing |
| Share wins | Celebrate others genuinely, not performatively |
| Ask questions | Start real conversations |

## Growth Tactics

### Phase 1: 0 to 1K Followers

| Tactic | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Thread daily | Build a content library fast |
| Reply to 20+ per day | Get noticed by target audience |
| Join engagement pods | Mutual engagement groups |
| DM connect | Build genuine relationships one by one |

### Phase 2: 1K to 10K Followers

| Tactic | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Collaborate | Guest threads, Twitter Spaces |
| Newsletter | Capture followers off-platform |
| Product drops | Build anticipation before launches |
| Controversy | Calculated hot takes with substance |

### Phase 3: 10K+ Followers

| Tactic | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Monetize | Products, sponsorships |
| Delegate | Scheduling, analytics |
| Diversify | Expand to other platforms |
| Community | Private groups and exclusive access |
