### Format 4: List Post with Actionable Takeaways

**Best for:** Monday or Wednesday
**Goal:** High saves and shares (bookmark-worthy content)

```
7 things I've learned building AI systems
for businesses (after 15+ client projects):

1. Start with the WORKFLOW, not the tech.
   Map the human process before automating it.

2. AI agents need GUARDRAILS, not freedom.
   Constrained agents outperform open-ended ones 10:1.

3. The first version will be wrong.
   Plan for 3 iterations minimum.

4. Data quality > model quality.
   GPT-4 with bad data loses to GPT-3.5 with clean data.

5. Measure TIME SAVED, not "AI accuracy."
   Clients care about hours reclaimed, not F1 scores.

6. Always build a human escalation path.
   100% automation is a myth. 85% is the sweet spot.

7. Document EVERYTHING.
   When the AI breaks (it will), documentation is your lifeline.

---

Which of these resonates most
with your experience?

Save this for later.
Share it with your team.

#AIImplementation #LessonsLearned #ASAAS

```

Gareth

```
Thanks for connecting, [Name].

I noticed [their company] works in [industry].

that [specific result - e.g., "cut support costs by 85%"].

Not pitching - just curious: what's your biggest
operational bottleneck right now?
```

**Message 3 (Day 7 if they replied):**

```
That's a common challenge in [industry].

using an AI agent team approach.

Would a 15-minute call be useful?
I can share exactly what we did
and whether it applies to your situation.

No pressure either way.

```
No worries if the timing isn't right, [Name].

are implementing AI agent systems.

If that's ever relevant, my content
might be useful. Happy to stay connected.

### Pricing Presentation Strategy

**Never lead with price on LinkedIn.** Build value first.

| Stage | What to share |
|-------|---------------|
| **Posts** | Results and ROI, never pricing |
| **DM conversations** | Range only: "Projects typically run $5K-$45K depending on scope" |
| **Discovery call** | Detailed scope + custom quote |
| **Proposal** | Full breakdown with ROI projection |

**Pricing anchoring in content:**

```
"Our client was spending $8,500/month on support.

We built an AI system for a one-time
investment of $12K.

ROI payback: 6 weeks.
Annual savings: $87,600."
```

This positions the price as an investment, not a cost.

### Objection Handling in Comments

| Objection | Response framework |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "AI isn't reliable enough" | "You're right that raw AI output needs guardrails. That's exactly what we build - not just AI, but AI SYSTEMS with validation, escalation, and human oversight. The 85% automation sweet spot." |
| "This is too expensive" | "I understand. The question is: what's the cost of NOT automating? If your team spends 20 hours/week on [task], that's $X/year in salary alone. Our systems typically pay for themselves in 6-8 weeks." |
| "We tried AI and it didn't work" | "Most AI implementations fail because they automate tasks, not workflows. We map the entire process first, then build systems that handle the full workflow - including edge cases and human escalation." |
| "Can't we just use ChatGPT?" | "ChatGPT is a tool. What we build are systems. The difference: ChatGPT answers one question. Our systems handle the entire workflow, 24/7, with no manual intervention needed." |
| "How do I know this will work for us?" | "Great question. We always start with a discovery call to understand your specific situation. If it's not a fit, I'll tell you. We've turned down projects where AI wasn't the right solution." |
