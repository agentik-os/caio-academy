## Complète Module Structure

### MODULE 1 — The New Search Landscape in 2026

**Objective**: The student understands how search has fundamentally changed and what it means for their strategy.

**Lessons to generate:**

1.1 **Search is no longer just Google**
- Google still dominates (~85% market share) BUT AI is eating into it
- New search surfaces: Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT web search, Perplexity, Bing Copilot, You.com
- Zero-click searches: 60%+ of Google searches don't result in a click
- The answer economy: people want answers, not links
- Your content must work for BOTH traditional search AND AI search

1.2 **How Google AI Overviews change SEO**
- Google now synthesizes answers from multiple sources at the top of results
- Being cited in AI Overviews = massive visibility (position 0+)
- What gets cited: structured, factual, well-sourced content
- What gets ignored: generic, thin, opinion-without-evidence content
- The new game: optimize for citability, not just rankings

1.3 **How AI search engines work (ChatGPT, Perplexity, etc.)**
- They crawl, synthesize, and cite — différent from Google's 10 blue links
- Getting cited = your brand mentioned in AI-generated answers
- AI crawlers: GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, etc.
- robots.txt strategy: ALLOW all AI crawlers (don't block your traffic source)
- llms.txt: the new standard for AI-readable site summaries

1.4 **The dual optimization framework**
- SEO (Search Engine Optimization): rank in Google's traditional results
- GEO (Generative Engine Optimization): get cited in AI-generated answers
- You need BOTH — they're complementary, not competing
- The good news: 80% of good SEO practices also help GEO

### MODULE 2 — Technical SEO Foundations

**Objective**: The student ensures their site is technically sound for search engines and AI crawlers.

**Lessons to generate:**

2.1 **Site architecture for SEO**
- URL structure: clean, descriptive, keyword-rich
- Hierarchy: home → category → page (max 3 clicks deep)
- Internal linking: every page should be reachable from at least 2 other pages
- Breadcrumbs: help users and search engines understand structure
- Navigation: clear, crawlable, mobile-friendly

2.2 **Core Web Vitals (the performance metrics Google cares about)**
- LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): main content visible in < 2.5s
- INP (Interaction to Next Paint): responds to input in < 200ms
- CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): visual stability < 0.1
- How to measure: PageSpeed Insights, Lighthouse, Web Vitals extension
- Common fixes: image optimization, font loading, JavaScript deferral

2.3 **Crawlability and indexability**
- robots.txt: what to allow, what to block
- Sitemap.xml: must include all important pages, exclude thin pages
- Canonical URLs: prevent duplicate content issues
- noindex/nofollow: when to use them (thin pages, admin pages)
- Redirect management: 301 for permanent, 308 for modern permanent
- HTTP headers: X-Robots-Tag, rel=canonical

2.4 **Structured data (Schema.org)**
- What it is: machine-readable metadata about your content
- Key schema types: Organization, WebPage, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList, Article, Product, LocalBusiness, Service
- Implementation: JSON-LD in the `<head>` (the recommended format)
- Validation: Google Rich Results Test, Schema.org validator
- Impact: rich snippets, knowledge panels, AI citations

2.5 **AI crawler optimization**
- Allow all AI crawlers in robots.txt (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, etc.)
- Create `/llms.txt` and `/llms-full.txt` for AI-readable site summaries
- Server-render content (no client-side-only rendering for important text)
- Structured data helps AI understand your content
- FAQ sections are gold for AI citation

2.6 **Mobile-first indexing**
- Google indexes the mobile version first
- Responsive design is non-negotiable
- Test: Google Mobile-Friendly Test
- Common issues: tiny text, untappable buttons, horizontal scroll

**Exercise**: Run a full technical SEO audit on your site. Fix all critical issues.

### MODULE 4 — AI Content Création at Scale

**Objective**: The student masters AI-assisted content création that ranks and converts.

**Lessons to generate:**

4.1 **The AI content création framework**
- AI writes the first draft (80% of the work)
- Human adds expertise, expérience, and opinion (E-E-A-T)
- AI optimizes for SEO (meta tags, structure, keywords)
- Human reviews and publishes
- This is NOT "let AI write everything" — it's AI-augmented human expertise

4.2 **Writing SEO-optimized articles with Claude**
- Prompt engineering for SEO content
- Article structure: answer-first, then depth
- Heading hierarchy: H1 → H2 → H3 (logical, keyword-rich)
- Word count: aim for 2000-3000 words for competitive topics
- Natural keyword integration (no stuffing)
- Internal and external linking
- FAQ sections (excellent for featured snippets + AI citation)

4.3 **Content formats that rank**

| Format | Best for | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Ultimate guide** | High-competition keywords | "Complète Guide to AI SEO 2026" |
| **Comparison** | Commercial intent | "Claude vs ChatGPT for Business" |
| **How-to** | Informational intent | "How to Set Up MCP Servers" |
| **List** | Broad topics | "15 Best AI Tools for Marketing" |
| **Case study** | Trust building | "How We Increased Traffic 300%" |
| **Glossary** | Long-tail keywords | "What is Agentic AI?" |
| **Tool page** | Commercial intent | "Free AI Audit Tool" |

4.4 **E-E-A-T: What Google actually wants**
- Expérience: show you've DONE what you're writing about
- Expertise: demonstrate deep knowledge
- Authoritativeness: be recognized in your field
- Trustworthiness: be accurate, cite sources, be transparent
- How to signal E-E-A-T in your content (author bios, case studies, data, citations)

4.5 **Avoiding AI content detection penalties**
- Google doesn't penalize AI content — they penalize LOW-QUALITY content
- Add personal expérience, unique data, original insights
- Don't publish raw AI output without human review
- Vary your content structure and voice
- Include original images, charts, and data

4.6 **Automated content pipelines**
- The Agentik OS approach: cron → Claude generates → SEO optimization → image génération → build → deploy → social posting → notification
- Setting up auto-publishing with Claude Code skills
- Quality gates: review before publish
- Scaling: 1 article/day on autopilot

**Exercise**: Write 3 SEO-optimized articles using the AI framework. Compare their quality with and without human editing.

### MODULE 5 — GEO: Generative Engine Optimization

**Objective**: The student optimizes their content for AI search engines.

**Lessons to generate:**

5.1 **What is GEO and why it matters**
- GEO = making your content citable by AI search engines
- AI engines cite content that is: factual, structured, well-sourced, definitive
- Being cited in ChatGPT/Perplexity/AI Overviews = brand visibility at scale
- It's the SEO equivalent of "position 0" but for AI

5.2 **The 7 GEO signals**

| Signal | What it means | How to optimize |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Citability** | Can AI quote your content directly? | Write clear, factual sentences that stand alone |
| **Structure** | Is your content well-organized? | Use headings, lists, tables, FAQ sections |
| **Authority** | Is your source trusted? | Build backlinks, citations, E-E-A-T |
| **Freshness** | Is the content current? | Update regularly, include dates |
| **Completeness** | Does it cover the topic thoroughly? | Be the most comprehensive source |
| **Uniqueness** | Do you offer unique insights? | Add original data, case studies, expérience |
| **Accessibility** | Can AI crawlers access it? | Allow AI bots, use server-rendering |

5.3 **llms.txt: Your AI-readable summary**
- What it is: a text file at `/llms.txt` that summarizes your site for AI crawlers
- Format: structured markdown with key information
- `/llms.txt` (brief) and `/llms-full.txt` (comprehensive)
- What to include: company info, services, key content, structured data

5.4 **Passage-level citability**
- AI engines cite PASSAGES, not pages
- Write self-contained paragraphs that answer specific questions
- Each paragraph should be independently meaningful
- Use the "Wikipedia test": could this paragraph be cited in an encyclopedia?

5.5 **FAQ optimization for AI**
- FAQ sections are the #1 source for AI citations
- Each Q&A should be self-contained (question + complète answer)
- Use the actual questions people search for
- Include numbers, dates, and specific facts
- Schema markup (FAQPage) for double benefit: Google + AI engines

5.6 **Platform-specific optimization**
- Google AI Overviews: structured data, E-E-A-T, comprehensive content
- ChatGPT: clear facts, quotable passages, authoritative sources
- Perplexity: recent content, strong citations, comprehensive answers
- Bing Copilot: Schema.org markup, Bing Webmaster optimization

**Exercise**: Audit 5 pages for GEO signals. Optimize them for AI citability.

### MODULE 6 — Programmatic SEO at Scale

**Objective**: The student can generate hundreds of SEO-optimized pages from templates and data.

**Lessons to generate:**

6.1 **What is programmatic SEO (pSEO)**
- Generate hundreds or thousands of pages from one template + data
- Example: Zapier = 16.2M monthly visitors from 1.3M keywords via pSEO
- Pattern: "[Tool A] + [Tool B] integration" × 1000 combinations
- NOT spam — each page must provide real, unique value

6.2 **pSEO page types that work**

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Comparison** | "[Product] vs [Product]" | "Claude Code vs Cursor" |
| **Integration** | "[Tool A] + [Tool B]" | "Slack + GitHub Integration" |
| **Location** | "[Service] in [City]" | "AI Consulting in Paris" |
| **Use case** | "[Service] for [Industry]" | "AI Agents for Healthcare" |
| **Glossary** | "[Term] definition" | "What is Agentic AI?" |
| **Alternative** | "[Product] alternatives" | "Zapier Alternatives 2026" |

6.3 **Building pSEO with Next.js**
- Dynamic routes: `[slug]/page.tsx` with `generateStaticParams()`
- Data files: JSON/TypeScript with structured content per page
- Unique content per page: AI-generated descriptions, FAQs, use cases
- Metadata: unique title, description, OG tags per page
- Internal linking: cross-link between related pages

6.4 **Content quality at scale**
- The thin content trap: don't generate 1000 pages with 100 words each
- Minimum viable content: 500+ words unique per page
- AI-generated enrichment: longDescription, FAQs, use cases, benefits
- Quality gates: check content length and uniqueness before indexing
- noindex for pages that don't meet quality threshold

6.5 **Measuring pSEO success**
- Google Search Console: impressions, clicks, indexed pages
- Coverage report: how many pages are indexed vs submitted
- Traffic per template: which page types perform best
- Conversion rate by page type

**Exercise**: Create a pSEO system with 50+ pages using a template. Deploy and track indexing.

### MODULE 7 — Link Building & Authority

| **Tool/calculator** | High | Very High | Very High |
| **Broken link building** | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| **Digital PR** | High | Very High | Medium |
| **Content partnerships** | Medium | High | Medium |

7.3 **AI-assisted link building**
- Claude for outreach email drafting
- AI for finding link opportunities (broken links, unlinked mentions)
- AI for personalization at scale

7.4 **Internal linking strategy**
- Every page links to 3-5 related pages
- Pillar pages link to all cluster articles and vice versa
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Audit regularly for orphan pages
