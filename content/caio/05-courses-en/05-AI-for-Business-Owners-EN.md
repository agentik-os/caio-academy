### MODULE 1 — AI Demystified: What Business Owners Actually Need to Know

**Objective**: The student understands what AI is, what it can and can't do, and why it matters NOW for their business.

**Lessons to generate:**

1.1 **What AI actually is (in plain English)**
- AI = pattern recognition + decision-making at scale
- The 3 types that matter for business: generative AI (creates), predictive AI (forecasts), agentic AI (acts)
- What AI is NOT: it's not magic, not sentient, not a replacement for strategy
- Analogy: AI is like electricity in 1920 — every business will use it, the question is how fast you adopt

1.2 **The AI landscape in 60 seconds**
- Large Language Models (Claude, GPT, Gemini): your new writing/thinking/analysis team
- Image/Video AI (Midjourney, Runway, Sora): your new créative department
- Automation AI (agents, workflows): your new operations team
- The key insight: these tools COMBINED replace entire departments

1.3 **Why 2026 is the inflection point**
- AI models became production-ready (reliable enough for real work)
- Orchestration tools matured (you can chain AI actions into workflows)
- Cost arbitrage is massive (AI team costs 1/10th of human team)
- Companies that adopt now get a 2-3 year head start
- Companies that wait will pay 10x more to catch up

1.4 **The real risks of AI (honest assessment)**
- Data privacy and security concerns
- Quality control (AI makes confident mistakes)
- Dependency risk (what if the tools change?)
- Legal landscape (copyright, liability)
- The #1 risk: doing nothing while competitors adopt

**Objective**: The student identifies specific, high-ROI AI opportunities in their own business.

**Lessons to generate:**

2.1 **The 7 areas where AI creates immédiate value**

| Area | What AI does | Typical ROI |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Customer support | Chatbots, ticket routing, response drafting | 60-80% cost reduction |
| Content création | Blog, social media, email, ads | 10x output, 1/5th cost |
| Sales | Lead scoring, outreach, proposal génération | 30-50% more pipeline |
| Operations | Workflow automation, data entry, reporting | 40-70% time savings |
| Development | Code génération, testing, deployment | 3-5x developer productivity |
| Marketing | SEO, ad optimization, market research | 2-4x ROI on marketing spend |
| Strategy | Competitive analysis, financial modeling, forecasting | Better decisions, faster |

2.2 **The AI audit framework (do it yourself)**
- Step 1: List every recurring task in your business (be exhaustive)
- Step 2: Score each task: repetitive? data-driven? time-consuming? expensive?
- Step 3: Map to AI capability (can AI do 80%+ of this task?)
- Step 4: Calculate ROI (current cost vs AI cost)
- Step 5: Prioritize by ROI and implementation difficulty

2.3 **Industry-specific AI opportunities**
- SaaS startups: automate development, QA, customer support, content
- E-commerce: product descriptions, personalization, inventory forecasting
- Professional services: proposal génération, client reporting, research

- Real estate: listings, market analysis, lead qualification
- Agencies: content production, campaign optimization, client delivery
- Manufacturing: quality control, predictive maintenance, supply chain

2.4 **What AI can't do (yet)**
- Tasks requiring genuine empathy and human connection
- Novel strategic thinking (AI extends your thinking, doesn't replace it)
- Physical tasks (AI is digital-only for now)
- Tasks requiring accountability (AI advises, humans decide)
- Highly regulated decisions (legal, medical diagnoses)

### MODULE 3 — The Economics of AI: What It Really Costs

**Objective**: The student understands AI costs, pricing models, and can build a realistic budget.

**Lessons to generate:**

3.1 **AI cost structure demystified**
- AI tools: $0-200/month per tool (SaaS subscriptions)
- AI development: $15K-45K per project (custom builds)
- AI leadership (CAIO): $4K-15K/month ongoing
- AI infrastructure: $5-50/month (servers, APIs)
- Total: $500-5,000/month for a small business, $5K-25K for mid-size

3.2 **Cost comparison: Traditional team vs AI system**

| Function | Traditional team | AI system | Annual savings |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|---------------|
| CTO + 3 devs | $400-600K/year | $8-15K/month | 70-85% |
| Marketing team (4) | $250-400K/year | $5-10K/month | 75-90% |
| QA team (2) | $150-250K/year | $3-5K/month | 80-90% |
| Customer support (3) | $120-200K/year | $2-5K/month | 85-95% |
| Content team (3) | $180-300K/year | $3-8K/month | 80-90% |

3.3 **How to budget for AI**
- Start small: $500-2K/month for tools and experimentation
- Phase 1 (Month 1-3): AI audit + first automation ($3-10K total)
- Phase 2 (Month 3-6): Build core systems ($15-45K total)
- Phase 3 (Month 6+): Ongoing optimization ($4-15K/month)
- The rule of thumb: budget 20-30% of what you'd pay for the human equivalent

3.4 **Pricing models you'll encounter**
- Per-seat (Jasper, Copy.ai): pay per user
- Usage-based (OpenAI API, Claude API): pay per token/request
- Flat monthly (most SaaS tools): predictable cost
- Project-based (custom development): one-time investment
- Retainer (CAIO partnership): ongoing strategic leadership

3.5 **Avoiding the money pits**
- Don't pay for enterprise when starter works
- Don't build custom when off-the-shelf exists
- Don't hire full-time AI staff when a CAIO partnership is cheaper
- Don't buy 10 tools when 3 cover 90% of your needs
- The #1 waste: paying for AI tools nobody uses

### MODULE 4 — Hiring and Partnering for AI

**Objective**: The student knows how to evaluate AI talent and choose the right engagement model.

**Lessons to generate:**

4.1 **The 4 ways to get AI capabilities**

| Model | Best for | Cost | Speed | Control |
|-------|----------|------|-------|---------|
| DIY (tools only) | Simple automations | Low ($0-500/mo) | Fast | Full |
| Freelancer | One-off projects | Medium ($5-20K) | Medium | Medium |
| CAIO partnership | Ongoing AI leadership | Medium ($4-15K/mo) | Fast | High |
| AI agency | Large-scale transformation | High ($20-100K+) | Slow | Low |
| In-house hire | Enterprise only | Very high ($150K+/yr) | Slow | Full |

4.2 **How to evaluate AI talent**
- Ask them to show results, not resumes
- Look for case studies with real numbers
- Ask: "What would you automate first in my business?"
- Red flags: promises of "AI will do everything", no specific methodology, no portfolio
- Green flags: starts with audit, shows ROI calculations, has production systems

4.3 **The CAIO model explained**
- What a CAIO (Chief AI Officer) does
- Why it's better than hiring full-time for most businesses
- The typical engagement: audit → build → ongoing partnership
- What to expect month by month
- How to measure success

4.4 **Questions to ask before signing**
- "What's your approach to the first 30 days?"
- "Can you show me a system you've built that's running in production?"
- "What happens to the systems if we stop working together?"
- "How do you measure ROI?"
- "What's your stack?" (Claude Code, MCP, etc. = good sign)

### MODULE 5 — AI Strategy: Building Your Roadmap

**Objective**: The student creates a concrete 90-day AI implementation roadmap for their business.

**Lessons to generate:**

5.1 **The 3-phase implementation framework**
- **Phase 1: Quick wins (Days 1-30)**: Deploy 2-3 AI tools for immédiate value
- **Phase 2: Core systems (Days 30-60)**: Build custom automations for your biggest pain points
- **Phase 3: Scale (Days 60-90)**: Optimize, expand, measure ROI, plan next quarter

5.2 **Quick wins any business can implement in Week 1**
- ChatGPT/Claude for email drafting, meeting summaries, research
- AI scheduling (Cal.com, Reclaim.ai)
- AI customer support (Intercom, Crisp with AI)
- AI content drafting (Claude for blogs, LinkedIn, newsletters)
- AI transcription (Descript, Otter.ai for meetings)

5.3 **Building your AI stack**
- Core: one AI assistant (Claude or ChatGPT)
- Content: one writing tool + one image tool
- Automation: one workflow tool (Zapier, Make, or n8n)
- Communication: one AI-enhanced CRM or support tool
- Analytics: one AI-powered analytics tool
- Total monthly cost: $100-500 for the basics

5.4 **Change management: Getting your team on board**
- Start with champions (find the 1-2 people who are excited about AI)
- Show, don't tell (demo results, don't lecture about potential)
- Make it easy (set up tools, create templates, provide training)
- Address fears honestly ("AI replaces tasks, not people — if you use it")
- Celebrate wins publicly

5.5 **Measuring AI ROI**
- Time saved (hours/week × hourly cost)
- Cost reduced (cancelled subscriptions, reduced headcount needs)
- Revenue gained (more leads, faster delivery, new capabilities)
- Quality improved (fewer errors, better consistency)
- The dashboard: track these 4 metrics monthly
